\frame
{
	\frametitle{Summary}
	\begin{itemize}
	\item By quantitatively comparing the data from different cuts, I found that distance to bad cells should be cut to greater than 1 and the distance to border, to greater than 0
	\item The  $\lambda_{02}$, dR, distance to border, and distance to bad cell cuts cut 41\%, 19\%, 7\%, and 26\% of the data, respectively
	\item The Ncells, exoticity, and cluster time cuts barely cut any data at all
	\end{itemize}
}
